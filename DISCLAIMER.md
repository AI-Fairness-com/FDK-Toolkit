# Legal and Ethical Disclaimer

## Important Distinction: Technical Fairness vs Legal Compliance

The FDK Toolkit is a **technical auditing tool**. It provides statistical fairness metrics (e.g., disparate impact ratios, equal opportunity differences, calibration gaps) and generates structured audit reports.

**The FDK Toolkit is NOT a legal compliance tool.**

Source: Chapter 1.1, p. 29

> *"FDK™'s unique value lies in providing an initial triage layer: it does not seek to replace expert resources, nor is it intended as a certification mechanism or a substitute for causal analysis, advanced statistical evaluation, or legal judgment."*

---

## What FDK Does

- Measures statistical disparities across demographic groups
- Generates reproducible audit trails (evidence packs)
- Provides plain-language summaries of findings
- Flags potential fairness concerns for human review
- Supports escalation to specialist teams for deeper analysis

---

## What FDK Does NOT Do

- **Does not** determine legal compliance with any specific regulation (GDPR, EU AI Act, Equality Act 2010, ECOA, etc.)
- **Does not** adjudicate discrimination claims
- **Does not** perform causal inference
- **Does not** replace human judgment or organizational governance
- **Does not** provide legal advice

Source: Chapter 8.7.2, p. 132–134

> *"FDK™ is designed to support decision-making, not replace it. Its role is to help professionals run fairness checks, structure results, and guide next steps. Final responsibility always remains with human decision-makers."*

> *"Some situations are not suitable for automated or no-code diagnostics. These include: legal disputes (e.g. discrimination claims), safety-critical decisions (e.g. clinical diagnosis, criminal justice), complex causal analysis requiring advanced modelling."*

---

## GREEN/YELLOW/RED Indicators

The traffic-light indicators (Green/Yellow/Red) in FDK outputs represent **statistical thresholds** defined in the methodology. They do **not** represent:

- Legal compliance or violation
- Ethical approval or condemnation
- Certification of fairness
- A final verdict on whether a system should be deployed

Source: Chapter 8.6, p. 126–128 (threshold mapping table)

**Action required upon receiving a RED or YELLOW indicator:**
- Human review is mandatory
- Escalation to governance bodies may be required
- Legal review may be necessary depending on jurisdiction

---

## Professional Responsibility

**Source: Chapter 8.7.2, p. 134**

> *"By making its own process transparent and by deferring complex cases to specialists, FDK™ supports accountability while ensuring that responsibility remains with people, not the tool."*

Users of FDK Toolkit are responsible for:

1. Understanding the limitations of the metrics they are using
2. Interpreting results within their domain context
3. Seeking legal advice when compliance questions arise
4. Documenting decisions and their rationale
